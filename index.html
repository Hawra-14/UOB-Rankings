Why This System Exists

University rankings don't evaluate themselves. Behind every QS or THE score is a year of data collection — hundreds of answers, documents, URLs, and evidence parcels gathered from across 12 departments. Without a coordinated system, critical data gets lost in email threads, submitted late, or never collected at all. This portal solves that.

1
Admins Assign, Departments Deliver
Each ranking question is routed to the right department with a deadline. No guesswork, no duplicate effort.
2
One Audited Record
Every submission is logged with timestamps and status — accepted, queried, or returned for revision. Nothing disappears.
3
Five Rankings, One Workflow
QS WUR, QS Sustainability, THE WUR, THE Impact, and UI GreenMetric are all managed here, each with its own cycle and indicator set.
QS · World University Rankings

QS World University Rankings

Published June Since 2004 1,500+ Universities

The world's most widely consulted university ranking. Evaluates institutions across 9 indicators combining global reputation surveys, Elsevier Scopus citation data, and internationalisation metrics.

📊 Indicators & Weights
▲
  • Academic Reputation — 30% · Global survey of 140,000+ academics
  • Citations per Faculty — 20% · Research impact via Elsevier Scopus
  • Employer Reputation — 15% · Survey of 100,000+ employers worldwide
  • Faculty / Student Ratio — 10% · Teaching capacity proxy
  • International Faculty & Students — 10% · Diversity and openness signal
  • Sustainability — 5% · ESG performance score
  • Employment Outcomes — 5% · Graduate employability index
  • International Research Network — 5% · Breadth of global research partnerships
🏛️ Key UoB Departments
▼
  • Office of the Vice-President for Academic Affairs
  • Scientific Research & Graduate Studies
  • Student Affairs & Registration
  • International Affairs Office
  • Quality Assurance Directorate
💡 What Makes a Strong Submission
▼
  • Evidence must be publicly accessible with a permanent URL
  • Co-authored papers must list UoB affiliation clearly
  • Employer engagements must name the organisation and date
  • International faculty data must include passport verification

THE · World University Rankings

THE World University Rankings

Published October Since 2010 2,000+ Universities

Times Higher Education's flagship ranking evaluates research-intensive universities across 5 pillars and 18 indicators. Research quality is measured using Field-Weighted Citation Impact (FWCI) from Elsevier Scopus — one of the most rigorous citation methodologies in global rankings.

📊 Five Pillars & Weights
▲
  • Research Quality — 30% · FWCI, research strength and excellence
  • Teaching — 29.5% · Reputation survey, staff/student ratio, PhDs awarded
  • Research Environment — 29% · Research income, productivity, and reputation
  • International Outlook — 7.5% · International staff, students, and co-authorship
  • Industry Income — 4% · Knowledge transfer and commercial research income
🏛️ Key UoB Departments
▼
  • Scientific Research & Graduate Studies
  • Finance & Budgeting Directorate
  • Industry Liaison & Innovation Office
  • International Affairs Office
💡 What Makes a Strong Submission
▼
  • Research income must be externally funded and audited
  • Industry partnerships must demonstrate knowledge transfer outcomes
  • Citation data sourced only from Elsevier Scopus — verify UoB affiliation strings

THE · Impact Rankings

THE Impact Rankings

Published April / May Since 2019 1,900+ Universities

The only global ranking measuring universities against all 17 UN SDGs. Score = SDG 17 Partnerships (22%, mandatory) + best 3 of the remaining 16 SDGs (78%). Open to any university — no minimum research requirement.

📊 How Scoring Works
▲
  • SDG 17 — Partnerships (22%) · Mandatory. Cross-sector collaboration, data sharing, policy engagement.
  • Best 3 remaining SDGs (78%) · UoB selects the 3 SDGs with strongest evidence to maximise score.
  • Each SDG scored on: research output + stewardship (policy/practice) + outreach (community engagement)
  • All evidence must have a permanent, publicly accessible URL
✓ Accepted vs ✗ Rejected
▼
  • ✓ Published research with UoB affiliation and public DOI
  • ✓ Official university policy with effective date and signatory
  • ✗ Internal memos or documents behind login walls
  • ✗ News articles without a direct UoB involvement statement
💡 Submission Template
▼
  • Title of initiative or research output
  • SDG(s) addressed (select all that apply)
  • Type: Research / Policy / Outreach / Partnership
  • Permanent URL — must remain accessible post-submission
  • Date of publication or implementation
UI GreenMetric · Campus Sustainability

UI GreenMetric World University Rankings

Published December Since 2010 1,477+ Universities · 95 Countries

The world's first campus sustainability ranking by Universitas Indonesia. Evaluates physical campus operations across 6 categories and 61 indicators. Everything must be measurable and campus-based. 2025 theme: "Doing SDGs in Higher Education."

📊 Six Categories & Weights
▲
  • Setting & Infrastructure — 15% · Green space, open land, forest area on campus
  • Energy & Climate Change — 21% · Renewable energy use, smart buildings, GHG emissions
  • Waste — 18% · Waste reduction policy, recycling programmes
  • Water — 10% · Conservation, recycling, consumption monitoring
  • Transportation — 18% · Shuttle buses, bicycle lanes, EV infrastructure, car-free zones
  • Education & Research — 18% · Sustainability courses, campus budget, student organisations
✓ Accepted vs ✗ Rejected
▼
  • ✓ Measured, campus-specific data with documented sources
  • ✓ Photos and plans of green infrastructure with timestamps
  • ✗ City-level data attributed to the university
  • ✗ Unverified estimates without measurement methodology
